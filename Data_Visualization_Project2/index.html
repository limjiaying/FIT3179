World Population

The world population currently stands at 7.98 billion , and is estimated to hit 8 billion by end of the year 2022. This total far exceeds the 2015 world population of 7.2 billion. The world's population continues to increase by roughly 140 people per minute, with births outweighing deaths in most countries.

World Population Percentage and Population Density

The population of Asia which is the largest continent with more than 40 countries has took up more than 50% of the world's population percentage. It is clear that India and China are the countries that contributes the most to this. While the smallest continent - Oceania with only 4 countries takes up less than 1% of the world population percentage.

The population density of a country is the number of population per unit of total land area of the country. It can affect a country in different ways in terms of economic growth and environmental impact. High Population Density can cause rapid economic and social development, however, on the downside, it may result in overcrowding which can lead to destruction of the country's ecosystem. Low Population Density can allow better nature exposure for the citizens, but may result in political and economic isolation, lack in health care and education, which discourages people from living in an area.

Population Growth Rate

Population growth rate is the annual average rate of change of population size in a country. The world's population continues to increase, with approximately 140 million babies born every year. The chart visualizes the top 10 countries with the highest growth rate at 2022. Most populated countries such as China and India are not included, however Moldova that is in the Europe has the highest growth rate in the world ranks at 135 in the world population ranking. This strongly indicates that country population is not proportional to growth rate, and vice versa.

Positive effects of population growth:
  • Economic boost
  • Improved trade structures
  • Increased innovation
  • Better social health
Negative effects of population growth:
  • Lower the speed of capital formation
  • Needs more investment
  • Negatively impavts the per Capital Income
  • Creates Unemployment
  • Leads to Food Problems
  • Give rise to poverty

Did COVID19 affect the world's population growth?

The diverging chart visualizes the changes in the average population growth in a year for some countries with the largest population before and after the pandemic. We can observe that the average population growth in most of the country including Malaysia and Australia had decreased after the world suffers from COVID19 in the year 2020.

The most significant change can be seen in China, where it is known to have the strictest lockdown during the pandemic. Countries such as Japan and Russia that has negative values that implies the decline in population. One main reason that caused this phenomena is the rate of death outweighs the rate of birth due to the COVID19 virus.

On the other hand, there are countries such as Nigeria and Pakistan, where their population still continued to rise even during the pandemic!
