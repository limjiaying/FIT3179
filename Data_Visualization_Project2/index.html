World Population

The world population currently stands at 7.98 billion , and is estimated to hit 8 billion by end of the year 2022. The world's population continues to increase by roughly 140 people per minute, with births outweighing deaths in most countries.

World Population Percentage and Population Density

The population of Asia which is the largest continent with more than 40 countries has took up more than 50% of the world's population percentage. It is clear that India and China are the countries that contributes the most to this. While the smallest continent - Oceania with only 4 countries takes up less than 1% of the world population percentage.

The population density of a country is the number of population per unit of total land area of the country. It can affect a country in different ways in terms of economic growth and environmental impact. High Population Density can cause rapid economic and social development, however, on the downside, it may result in overcrowding which can lead to destruction of the country's ecosystem. Low Population Density can allow better nature exposure for the citizens, but may result in political and economic isolation. health care, which discourages people from living in an area.
